APP5 · Europe time-use dataset

Europe’s day, redrawn by age

A desktop reading of the whole of Europe, tracing how a curated basket of daily activities shifts across generations from 2000 to 2020.

How to read it

Start with the flowing ribbons to see the pecking order change, switch to the detail view for one category at a time, then finish with the social-context waffles showing alone, shared, and other time.

Coverage Whole of Europe
Years 2000 · 2010 · 2020
Age groups 10–24 · 25–44 · 45–64 · 65+

1 / Category flow

Which activities rise to the surface?

The area bump chart reorders categories every year from most to least prominent. Ribbon thickness reflects each activity’s share within the displayed activity basket for the selected age group.

2 / Detailed comparison

One category, all age groups

Select a category to compare its share of a 24-hour day across age groups. End labels keep the lines readable without forcing a separate key lookup.

Stable background categories such as sleeping and eating stay visible in the bump chart but are omitted from the default selector so smaller shifts remain legible.

Category share of a 24-hour day

3 / Social context

How time is socially distributed

Each waffle represents one year with 100 cells. The split follows the alone/shared/other grouping intent from the unified ACL notes, ignoring categories marked as none.

100 cells = 100% of time classified as alone, shared, or other
