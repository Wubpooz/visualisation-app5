Time Spend Analysis

As a social species how is the loneliness epidemic happening and can we see it in the evolution of our time use?

Simulated data - years 2000, 2010, 2020 - age groups 18-25, 25-35, 35-45

This visualisation explores how people across different age groups distribute their daily time across nine activity categories (eating, study, sleeping, paid work, household chores and family care, personal care, voluntary work, social life, travel and leisure) over three survey waves: 2000, 2010, and 2020.

Each data point encodes: the activity category, the age group (18-25, 25-35, 35-45), the survey year, the total time spent in hours, and the social context of that time (alone, shared with others, or other).

Mark: line + dot. X axis: survey year (quantitative). Y axis: total hours spent on the selected activity (quantitative, domain 0-10 h). Color (stroke / fill): age group - one line per age group, allowing comparison of how the same activity evolved differently across generations over time. Tooltip: exact hours on hover. Text label: age group name anchored to the last data point.

Time repartition : Alone vs Shared

Filtered by selected category and selected age group.

Mark: stacked bar (barY). X axis: survey year (ordinal band). Y axis: percentage of time (0-100 %). Color (fill): social context - Alone, Shared, Other - stacked to always reach 100 %, making it easy to spot whether the selected activity is becoming more solitary or more social over time. Tooltip: exact percentage per segment on hover.

Repartition of time spend (Year 2020)

Distribution of all activities for the selected year, faceted by age group.

Mark: waffle (waffleY) - each cell represents 1 % of daily time. Facet (fx): age group - one 10x10 waffle grid per group, enabling at-a-glance comparison of how priorities differ between generations. Color (fill): activity category (Tableau10 palette). Y (multiple = 10): percentage of total daily time allocated to each activity. Tooltip: activity name and percentage on hover.

Activity distribution — pie chart (Year 2020)

Same data as the waffle chart — year controlled by the selector above.

Mark: arc (D3 pie layout). Facet: one pie per age group (18-25, 25-35, 35-45). Angle: percentage of total daily time per activity. Color (fill): activity category (Tableau10 palette). Tooltip: category name and percentage on hover.
